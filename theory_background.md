## 一、理论背景

### 1. 威权舆论与“表态成本理论”（Cost of Expression）

在中国式数字公共空间中，个体面对的不是“是否同意”某种政治表达，而是**是否愿意公开展示同意**。
 —— UI 设计改变了这种“表态成本”：

- 私密的“点赞”是**低风险的私人认同（private assent）**；
- 半公开的“好看/在看/推荐”是**中等风险的半公开顺从（semi-public conformity）**；
- “分享”是**高风险的公开表态（public conformity）**。

因此，每次微信改版都改变了个人在政治传播中的心理边界。

### 2. “平台治理与行为诱导”（Platform Governance as Nudging）

腾讯的 UI 改动，本质上是对行为路径的“算法引导（nudge）”：

- 通过改变操作选项的**可见度**与**语义框架**，政府与平台可以间接地引导公众从“沉默”到“点在看”，从“点在看”到“转发”。
- 你可以用它来测试：**威权政体如何通过平台设计塑造表面支持而非内心信仰**。

## 三、核心假设（Hypotheses）

### 阶段一：2017 “看一看”上线——**算法与社交扩散的诞生**

**理论含义：** 信息流让公众暴露在更多“朋友认可的”官方内容中，形成“社会证明效应”（social proof）。
 **假设：**

1. （H1a）“看一看”上线后，公众号文章平均阅读量显著上升。
2. （H1b）传播尾部更长：文章在发布72小时后的增长率上升。
3. （H1c）民生/情感型内容比意识形态内容获得更大增长。
4. （H1d）这标志着威权传播从“中心推送”转向“去中心社交传播”机制。

------

### 阶段二：2018 “点赞→好看”——**公开化与从众机制的建立**

**理论含义：** 平台把原本的“私密认同”改为“好友可见”的行为，增加了“政治可见性成本”。这让用户必须在“表达真实态度”与“维持安全距离”之间权衡。
 **假设：**

5. （H2a）UI 改版后，整体“好看率”（每阅读的好看数）下降，但“分享率”上升。
6. （H2b）意识形态内容的“好看率”下降幅度更大（公众更谨慎表态）。
7. （H2c）服务性内容的“好看率”上升，说明公众更愿意公开关联“安全内容”。
8. （H2d）在高风险地区（清朗行动频繁的省份），“好看率”下降更明显。
9. （H2e）改版后几个月内“好看率”逐步回升，体现**表态习惯的适应**——即“威权下的表面顺从常态化”。

------

### 阶段三：2020 “点赞回归”——**表达分层与真实认同**

**理论含义：** 当私密渠道重新出现，用户可以选择风险更低的“赞”来表达内心态度；“在看”则继续承担半公开传播功能。
 **假设：**

10. （H3a）“在看率”下降，但“点赞率”上升（替代效应）。
11. （H3b）总参与率（赞+在看+分享）上升（互补效应：降低门槛激活沉默者）。
12. （H3c）意识形态内容更多获得“赞”而非“在看”，服务内容则相反。
13. （H3d）高审查地区的替代效应更强——风险越高，越倾向私密表达。
14. （H3e）私密表达增加但分享不变，表明真实认同（private persuasion）存在，而公开顺从（public conformity）并未增加。

Here’s the clean, final version of **four clear, falsifiable hypotheses** — grounded in your visibility timeline (2013–2025) and theoretical focus on *expression costs under authoritarianism*.

## **Research Question**

**How do changes in the visibility of political expression affect citizens’ willingness to signal political attitudes under authoritarian rule?**

------

### **H1 – Visibility Suppression Hypothesis**

When the visibility of political engagement increases (likes/“在看” become semi-public in 2017), **citizens reduce visible support for politically sensitive content**.

> **Expectation:** For propaganda posts, “在看” counts **decrease** immediately after May 18 2017, compared with neutral or service posts.

------

### **H2 – Private Substitution Hypothesis**

When visible endorsement becomes risky, users **shift engagement into low-visibility channels** (e.g., private reading, forwarding, or silent consumption).

> **Expectation:** After May 18 2017, **views and private forwards increase** for propaganda posts as “在看” falls; after July 15 2020 (when “在看” becomes private again), this pattern **reverses**.

------

### **H3 – Preference Falsification Gap Hypothesis**

Increased visibility **widens the gap between private and public engagement**; reducing visibility **narrows it**.

> **Expectation:** The ratio or difference between private actions (views + forwards) and semi-public actions (“在看”) **rises** in 2017 and **falls** in 2020, especially for propaganda content.

------

### **H4 – Political-Risk Gradient Hypothesis**

Visibility effects are **strongest where political risk is highest**.

> **Expectation:** The 2017 suppression and 2020 rebound are larger for
>  • central or provincial CCP accounts (vs. local service accounts),
>  • ideological or nationalist content (vs. routine administrative info),
>  • urban or high-surveillance regions.

------

These four hypotheses let you isolate **how visibility (i.e., expression cost)** shapes political signaling.

- **H1** captures suppression,
- **H2** captures behavioral substitution,
- **H3** quantifies falsification,
- **H4** shows heterogeneity by political risk.

Together, they form a full causal chain from *platform visibility → perceived expression cost → signaling behavior → regime stability*.



When the state raises the visibility of political expression, citizens face dual risks: state sanction for dissent and social scrutiny for over-conformity. As a result, even regime supporters suppress visible endorsement of propaganda, producing a behavioral equilibrium of muted signaling rather than overt loyalty. This extends Kuran’s model from one-sided falsification of dissent to a symmetric logic of expressive caution under surveillance.
